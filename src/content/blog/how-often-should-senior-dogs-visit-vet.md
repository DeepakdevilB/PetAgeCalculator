When your dog is young and healthy, an annual wellness exam is usually sufficient. But once they cross the threshold into their senior years (typically around age 7 for most breeds), everything changes.

Veterinarians strongly recommend bi-annual (twice a year) checkups for senior dogs. Because dogs age much faster than humans, a lot can happen in six months. Early detection through routine blood work is the key to treating age-related diseases successfully.
