It's one of the great tragedies of the dog world: the bigger the dog, the shorter the lifespan. Giant breeds like Great Danes, Mastiffs, and Irish Wolfhounds age at a highly accelerated rate compared to smaller dogs.

A Great Dane is considered a senior by age 5, and their average lifespan is just 7-10 years. Scientists believe this is due to the rapid cell division required to reach their massive adult size, which accelerates the aging process at a cellular level.
